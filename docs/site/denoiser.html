Denoising Pipeline

The MHRQI denoiser compares each pixel's intensity to its parent block average. Pixels that deviate significantly are marked in the outcome qubit, flagging them for smoothing during reconstruction.

Key Insight: Real edges are consistent at multiple hierarchy levels. Noise appears random and inconsistent with parent averages.

Denoising Algorithm (5 Steps)

Step 1: Sibling Superposition

Apply Hadamard gates to the finest-level position qubits. This creates a superposition over 4 sibling pixels that share the same parent block.

H(q_y_finest) ⊗ H(q_x_finest)

Step 2: Parent Average Encoding

Encode a weighted average of sibling intensities into an ancilla qubit using controlled-RY rotations. The MSB bits contribute most:

$$ CRY(\pi/16, I_7 \rightarrow ancilla) $$ $$ CRY(\pi/8, I_6 \rightarrow ancilla) $$ $$ CRY(\pi/4, I_5 \rightarrow ancilla) $$ $$ CRY(\pi/2, I_4 \rightarrow ancilla) $$

Step 3: Uncompute Superposition

Apply Hadamard gates again to collapse the sibling superposition back to individual pixels. The ancilla now holds the average encoded in its rotation angle.

Step 4: XNOR Comparison

Compare the pixel's MSB with the parent average using XNOR logic (CCX gates). If they match, the pixel is consistent. If not, it's flagged.

consistency = (pixel_MSB == parent_avg_MSB)

Step 5: Mark Outcome Qubit

Transfer the comparison result to the outcome qubit:

Outcome = 0: Pixel is consistent → preserve during reconstruction
Outcome = 1: Pixel is inconsistent → smooth with neighbors
