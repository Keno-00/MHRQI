MHRQI

A Hierarchical Representation for Quantum Image Processing v2.0.4

MHRQI (Magnitude-Hierarchical Representation of Quantum Images) is a framework developed to address spatial localization and noise challenges in quantum image processing. This project, focused on B-SCAN Retinal OCT images, explores the use of hierarchical trees to represent pixel data.

Traditional representations often treat an image as a flat bitstring, where local changes can impact the global state representation. MHRQI decomposes the image into a recursive quad-tree structure using hierarchical qubit registers, allowing for localized operations and hierarchical consistency checks.

The pipeline consists of five primary stages: coordinate remapping (HCV), circuit initialization, basis-encoded intensity upload, unitary consistency marking (denoising), and statistical image retrieval. Each stage is designed to be implemented as a reversible quantum circuit.

Current results indicate that hierarchical representations can effectively manage certain types of speckle noise common in medical imaging, though the implementation remains limited to classical simulations and small-scale quantum hardware constraints.
